Hello and welcome to Yaba: Yet Another Budgeting App.
This application was constructed using AngularJS, and a little bit of Google Charts to render your transactions to you in a way that makes sense. The idea is that you should be able to access the app, download your CSV files from your bank, unplug the ethernet cable and be able to do your budgeting. No big brother, no tracking, no logins, no excessive security requirements, no MFA: just you and your data.

This app is still very much under construction, but enough of it has been built that a demonstration of some pages can be rendered for folks to checkout and preview. Please understand that this has not passed a security audit or check. This application is for demonstration and experimentation purposes only.

All pages are currently "Functional". I use this term loosely because technically, you can navigate the flows and do what the app was intended to accomplish: Balance your budgets. There is still a ton of work to be done to further enhance the aesthetics [1]. However, the base functionality is something I have been driving to completion for about three months now (as of 2023-02-01).

You may notice the app doesn't make web requests to the Internet other than the HTML, CSS and JS that is required to make this app function. The security policy on the page should be only permissive enough for the app to behave as required (for running scripts and styles where expected). It will also use localStorage to store all data locally rather than sending it to some server. I have done some testing and can estimate that might be roughly 9999 transactions, but your mileage may vary. This was a long 256 character description and details filled out for every field. This was also strictly transactions and didn't include institutions and accounts. The real limit is the 5 Megabytes of storage your browser will allow this app to store in localStorage. The information is JSON encoded and stored with your browser.

With that said, there are some things that need to be mentioned: While this app does not talk to the Internet, I haven't thought out all the ways to keep the Internet from talking to this app. Data stored with this app is not encrypted. These are all wishlist items that I plan on rolling out if this explodes into interest that would require me to think about these things. In the meantime, I am focusing on a product that is useful to me and does what I need. I am simply providing a copy of this tool to you to learn for yourself if it's helpful to you.

Just don't be dumb and use this app on a public computer or visit strange websites pretending to be this app or give money to anyone trying to impersonate me thinking that you'll win a quick buck because I wrote a budgeting app. I'll never reach out to you for any money games and you can always find my official links on my blog at blog.markizano.net.

If you want to try this yourself and not use it on a public system, then simply clone or download the repository at github.com/markizano/yaba and visit the public/index.html in your browser. It should still work the same visiting the file directly.
